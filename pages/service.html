Peer Teacher

I work for Texas A&M University as a Peer Teacher (PT). I understand that computer science concepts can often be new and challenging for many students, especially if they haven't been introduced to them before college. That's where PTs like me come in. We have a solid grasp of these computer science concepts and ideas, which allows us to provide personalized guidance to our fellow students. We are well-equipped to help students overcome any roadblocks they encounter in their work. When students face challenges, we serve as valuable resources, offering insights and solutions to navigate their academic journey. What sets PTs apart is that we've walked in the same shoes as the students we mentor. We understand the unique challenges and opportunities of these courses, and we're here to offer not only academic guidance but also encouragement and advice on how to succeed. Many freshmen find us more approachable than professors or teaching assistants (TAs), making it easier for them to seek help and thrive in their studies.
